What Is a Spinal Adjustment?

A spinal adjustment — also called spinal manipulation — is the core of chiropractic care. It involves applying a controlled, precise force to specific joints of the spine that have become restricted, misaligned, or are not moving properly. When a spinal joint is stuck or out of position, it can irritate nearby nerves, cause pain, and reduce your body's ability to communicate and heal effectively.

Chiropractic spinal adjustment is one of the most studied forms of conservative care for musculoskeletal pain. A 2022 review published in Frontiers in Pain Research (PMC8915715) found strong evidence supporting spinal manipulation for both acute and chronic neck and back pain, with effectiveness comparable to — and in many cases better than — medication or surgery, and with a significantly safer risk profile.

At Dr. Wright Chiropractic, adjustments are never one-size-fits-all. Every correction is targeted to exactly what your body needs that day.

KST — Koren Specific Technique

Dr. Wright's primary adjustment method is KST (Koren Specific Technique), a revolutionary approach developed in 2003 by Dr. Tedd Koren, D.C. after years of clinical research. KST uses a small, handheld instrument called the ArthroStim to deliver precise, low-force corrections to specific vertebrae and other joints — without any twisting, popping, or forceful thrusting.

Here's what makes KST different:

  • Uses binary biofeedback to locate exact problem areas
  • Instrument-based — no manual manipulation
  • No twisting, cracking, or popping
  • Safe for all ages — infants to elderly
  • Can address the full spine and extremities
  • Often provides relief in fewer visits

KST's biofeedback method allows Dr. Wright to communicate directly with your nervous system — asking the body where it needs correction rather than relying on guesswork. The result is a highly individualized adjustment that addresses what your body needs on that specific day.

Many patients report feeling significant improvement after their very first KST session. It is especially well-suited for people who are nervous about traditional "cracking" adjustments, patients with osteoporosis or joint fragility, children and seniors, and anyone dealing with chronic or stubborn conditions that haven't responded to other care.

What Conditions Are Treated?

Spinal adjustments address a wide range of conditions rooted in spinal dysfunction, nerve irritation, and musculoskeletal imbalance. Common conditions Dr. Wright treats with spinal adjustments include:

  • Lower back pain
  • Upper back pain
  • Neck pain and stiffness
  • Tension headaches
  • Migraines
  • Sciatica
  • Herniated discs
  • Shoulder pain
  • Hip and pelvis pain
  • Numbness and tingling
  • Poor posture
  • Sports injuries

Beyond pain relief, regular spinal adjustments support improved nervous system function, better posture, increased range of motion, and reduced inflammation — all of which contribute to overall health and quality of life.

What the Research Says

Chiropractic spinal manipulation has been studied extensively and is now recommended by multiple major medical guidelines for musculoskeletal pain. Key findings include:

  • In a landmark study, 57% of chiropractic patients reported at least 75% pain reduction after 12 weeks — compared to 33% of those taking medication (ACA)
  • Medicare patients receiving chiropractic care had lower overall costs and shorter pain episodes than those in other treatment groups
  • A 2022 randomized controlled trial (PMC12697962) found 12 weeks of chiropractic adjustments produced measurable improvements in physiological biomarkers related to inflammation and immune function
  • The American College of Physicians recommends spinal manipulation as a first-line conservative treatment for acute and chronic low back pain before medication

The safety profile of chiropractic adjustments — particularly gentle instrument-based methods like KST — is excellent. Serious adverse events are extremely rare, and the risk is far lower than long-term pain medication use or surgery.
