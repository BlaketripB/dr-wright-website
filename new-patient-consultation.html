New Patient Consultation

Your first visit to Dr. Wright's office is about more than just your back. It's a comprehensive conversation about your health, your goals, and how chiropractic can help you feel your best.

Your First Visit — What to Expect

Your new patient visit begins before you even sit down with Dr. Wright. You'll start by completing intake paperwork that covers your health history, current symptoms, any medications you're taking, prior injuries, and your personal health goals. This information gives Dr. Wright a meaningful head start in understanding your situation before the conversation begins.

From there, you'll move into a thorough one-on-one consultation — the heart of the new patient experience. Dr. Wright will listen carefully to your story, ask thoughtful follow-up questions, and take the time to truly understand what you're experiencing, not just physically but in the context of your overall life and wellbeing. There is no clock ticking in the corner. Many patients note that this approach feels genuinely different from the rushed, abbreviated appointments they've experienced in conventional medical settings.

After the consultation comes the physical evaluation — a structured assessment designed to give Dr. Wright the clearest possible picture of your spinal health and how your body is functioning.

"Dr. Wright takes the time that most doctors don't. He listens, asks questions, and treats you as an individual — not a symptom on a chart."

The Physical Evaluation

Dr. Wright performs a thorough assessment of your posture, spinal alignment, and movement patterns. Rather than simply treating where it hurts, his evaluation is designed to uncover the underlying cause of your symptoms — a distinction that makes a significant difference in the quality and durability of your results.

Using Muscle Response Analysis and KST-based biofeedback, Dr. Wright can identify not just where you're experiencing pain, but why your body is responding the way it is. This level of assessment looks beyond surface-level relief and gets to the root of what's going on in your spine and nervous system.

The evaluation may include:

  • Posture assessment
  • Range of motion testing
  • Spinal palpation
  • Muscle Response Analysis
  • Neurological assessment as appropriate

The entire evaluation is completely non-invasive, comfortable, and respectful of your body's current state. You will never be pushed past what is appropriate for where you are right now.

Your Personalized Care Plan

After the evaluation, Dr. Wright will walk you through his findings in clear, plain language — no confusing medical jargon. He'll explain exactly what he found, what it means for your health, and what he recommends going forward. You'll leave the conversation understanding your own body better than you did when you walked in.

Your care plan is built specifically for you. It takes into account your condition, health history, age, lifestyle, and the goals you shared during the consultation. No two care plans look alike, because no two patients are alike.

For many patients, the first chiropractic adjustment can begin at the end of the initial consultation visit — so you don't have to wait to start feeling better. Your care plan may include:

  • A series of initial adjustment visits
  • At-home guidance on posture and movement
  • Nutritional recommendations if relevant
  • A long-term maintenance schedule once your primary goals are reached

Is Chiropractic Right for You?

Chiropractic care is appropriate for a wide range of people and conditions. The new patient consultation is also an opportunity for Dr. Wright to ensure you're a good candidate for care — and to refer you to the appropriate specialist if chiropractic is not the right fit for your situation. Dr. Wright's first priority is always your wellbeing, not filling an appointment slot.

Most adults can benefit from chiropractic evaluation. It is appropriate for:

  • Back pain and neck pain
  • Headaches and migraines
  • Sports and activity injuries
  • Post-accident recovery
  • General wellness and prevention
  • Reduced mobility and flexibility

If you are currently under the care of another healthcare provider, Dr. Wright welcomes coordinating care to ensure you receive the most comprehensive support possible. Collaboration is always an option.
